Sir Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill

Time magazine's "Man of the 20th Century?"

(Well, should have been.)

Here's a Time Line of Sir Winston Churchill's Life:

  • 1874 - Born at Blenheim Palace, Oxforshire, England, U.K. to the Rt. Hon. Lord Randolph Churchill, 3rd son of 7th Duke of Marlborough, and Jennie Jerome.
  • 1888 - 1893 - Attended Harrow School
  • 1893 - 1894 - Cavalry cadet at Sandhurst Royal Military Academy
  • 1904 - Moved from Conservative Party to Liberal Party, 31 May
  • 1907 Became a Privy Councilor
  • 1908 - Married, 12 September, to Clementine, daughter of Sir Henry Hozier and Lady Blanche (née Ogilvy)
  • 1924 - Returned to Conservative Party from Liberal Party
  • 1940 - 1945 - Prime Minister, First Lord of the Treasury and Minister of Defence
  • 1946 - "Iron Curtain" Speech, Fulton, Missouri, 5 March
  • 1951 - 1955 - Prime Minister and First Lord of the Treasury
  • 1953 - Awarded Nobel Prize for Literature
  • 1965 - Died, 24 January
